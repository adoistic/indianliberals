Inflation is the cruellest tax of all. It is a tax which is levied without the consent of the taxpayer, without the knowledge of the legislator, and without any clear notion of who pays it. It strikes hardest at those least able to defend themselves — pensioners, savers, salaried employees on fixed incomes, and the poor who hold what little wealth they have in currency.

The remedy is not the elaboration of more controls, nor the deeper involvement of the State in directing the economy, nor any of the planning expedients which have characterised the present decade. The remedy is the restoration of monetary discipline.
