Professor Bellikoth Ragunath Shenoy (1905–1978) was a classical liberal economist. Born on June 3, 1905, near Mangalore, Karnataka, he was educated at Benares Hindu University (where he secured a first-class first at the MA Economics Exam in 1929) and at the London School of Economics, where he was inspired by the ideas of Professor Friedrich Hayek.

During his LSE stint, two of his papers — "An Equation for the Price Level of New Investment Goods" (1931) and "Interdependence of Price Levels" (1933) — appeared in the *Quarterly Journal of Economics*. He was perhaps the first Indian economist to have a paper published in a leading scholarly journal.

In 1954, Shenoy joined Gujarat University as the first Director of its School of Social Sciences. During this period he made substantial contributions to Indian economic policy debates, most notably his "Note of Dissent to the Second Five Year Plan" — a foundational document of Indian classical liberalism.

He was a member of the internationally prestigious Mont Pelerin Society. After leaving Gujarat University in 1968, he founded the Economic Research Centre in Delhi and tirelessly espoused the cause of liberalism in India until his death on 8th February 1978.
