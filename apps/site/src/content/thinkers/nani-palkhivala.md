Nanabhoy "Nani" Ardeshir Palkhivala (1920–2002) was born in Bombay. His higher education at St Xavier's College was a master's degree in English. He earned his law degree at Government Law College, Bombay.

Palkhivala began his law practice in 1946 in Bombay. His initial expertise lay in commercial and tax law. In 1950, he authored with Sir Jamshedji *The Law and Practice of Income Tax*, which still serves as a reference guide and was also used as the tax law draft guide by the IMF.

Over time, Palkhivala cemented his reputation as one of India's most eminent jurists and an expert in economic affairs. His knowledge on matters related to the constitution and tax matters was unparalleled. His legacy in Indian democracy lies in his contribution to the famous Kesavananda Bharati vs. The State of Kerala case (1973), which ensured that the basic structure of the Indian Constitution cannot be altered by the legislature.
