Minoo Masani (1905–1998) was a co-founder of the Swatantra Party, editor of *Freedom First*, and one of India's foremost classical liberal voices through the 20th century. He argued for liberal democracy against both Nehruvian socialism and communist totalitarianism.

The Nagpur Resolution of Congress in 1959, which advocated cooperative farming, brought Rajaji and Masani together to form the Swatantra Party. The Swatantra Party came into being in 1959 as a coalition of pro-market businessmen, peasant proprietors, beleaguered princes, and zamindars. Masani served as General Secretary from 1959 to 1968 and was then elected the party President.

Swatantra emerged as the single largest opposition party in the fourth Lok Sabha (1967–1971) with 44 seats. After Swatantra's defeat, Masani retired from active politics but continued to manage DRS and *Freedom First*. He fought against the press censorship imposed during the Emergency. Project for Economic Education and the Leslie Sawhny Programme of Training for Democracy were his brainchildren for creating a liberal discourse in India.

He passed away in 1998 at the age of 93.
