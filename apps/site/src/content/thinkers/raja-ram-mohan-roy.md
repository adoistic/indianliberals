Raja Ram Mohan Roy (1772–1833) was a Bengal reformer and founder of the Brahmo Samaj — the intellectual progenitor of the Indian liberal tradition. He campaigned against sati, for women's education, for free press, and for English-language modern education in India.

He was honoured by the Mughal emperor Akbar II with the title "Raja". He travelled to England in 1830 as an envoy of the emperor, where he advocated for Indian rights and died in Bristol in 1833.
