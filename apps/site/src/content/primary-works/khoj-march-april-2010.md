- 2010 43 - Life, Liberty , Property - that is what Man is. Of these three things one may say , without any demagogic quibbling, that they are anterior and superior to all human legislation. It is not because men have passed laws that life, liberty and property exist.On the contrary, it is because life, liberty and property already exist that men make laws. Frederic Bastiat, The Law , 1850 (T)he conception of the Rule of the Law is sometimes confused with the requirement of mere legality in all government action. The Rule of the Law, of course, presupposes complete legality , but this is not enough: if a law gave the government unlimited power to act as it pleased, all its actions ainly not be under the Rule of the Law . The Rule of the Law requires that all laws conform to certain principles. F . A. Hayek, The Constitution of Liberty , 1960 The future is very open and depends upon us, on all of us. It depends on what you and I and many other people do, today, tomorrow and the day af ter tomorrow . And what we do depends in turn on our ideas and wishes, on our hopes and fears. It depends on how we see the world, and how we assess the open possibilities of the future.This means we have a great responsibility, which becomes even greater when we realize the following truth: we know nothing, or rather , we know so little that we can safely define that little as 'nothing', because it is nothing in comparison with what we would need to know to make the right decisions.Karl Popper , The Lesson of this Century , 1997
